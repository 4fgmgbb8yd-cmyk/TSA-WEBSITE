Arthur B. Newton (1864-1940)

Arthur B. Newton is often referred to as the “Godfather of Winter Garden” because of his major role in organizing and developing the early city. He opened the first store and helped establish the first newspaper, giving residents access to goods and local information.

In 1908, Newton played a key role in helping Winter Garden officially incorporate as a city and later served as its first mayor. His leadership helped structure the young government and guide early infrastructure development. Because of his influence, he remains one of the most important figures in Winter Garden’s founding.

Luther F. Tilden (1834-1929)

Luther F. Tilden was a successful citrus grower whose investments in orange groves helped establish Winter Garden as part of Florida’s booming citrus industry. His agricultural work contributed significantly to the region’s early economic growth and prosperity.

Beyond farming, Tilden’s influence is still visible through his historic home, Meadow Marsh. His leadership and business success represent the agricultural foundation upon which Winter Garden was built. His legacy reflects the importance of citrus to the city’s early years.

Juanita Coney Maxey (1906-2010)

Juanita Coney Maxey was an important civil rights leader and educator in East Winter Garden. She became the first African American woman to register and vote in the city after voting restrictions were removed. Her courage and determination symbolized progress during a period of segregation and discrimination.

Maxey also worked to improve educational opportunities for African American students and strengthen her community. Her leadership helped bring attention to equality and representation in Winter Garden. Today, she is remembered as a pioneer who advanced civil rights locally.

George B. McMillan(1916-1944)

George Bray McMillan was born in 1916 and raised in Winter Garden during the town’s early agricultural years. He earned an appointment to the U.S. Military Academy at West Point before joining the U.S. Army Air Forces in World War II. His path from a small Florida town to military service reflected both ambition and dedication.

McMillan later flew combat missions in China as part of the American Volunteer Group, famously known as the Flying Tigers. He was credited with multiple aerial victories and rose to the rank of lieutenant colonel at a young age. In 1944, he was killed in action, and he remains one of Winter Garden’s most honored wartime heroes.

Mildred Dixon (1923-2006)

Mildred Dixon made history as the first African American elected to the Winter Garden City Commission and the first woman elected in West Orange County. Her election marked a significant milestone in representation within local government.

During her time in office, Dixon worked to improve city services and ensure fair representation for residents. Her leadership demonstrated the growing role of women and minorities in shaping Winter Garden’s future. She remains a symbol of civic progress in the city.

Charles Alexander King

Charles Alexander King became the first African American police officer in Winter Garden in 1967. His appointment came during a time of important civil rights changes across the country. His role marked an important step toward greater diversity within local institutions.

King’s service helped break racial barriers in the city’s law enforcement system. By serving his community with professionalism and dedication, he opened doors for future generations. His career reflects Winter Garden’s gradual progress toward equality and inclusion.
